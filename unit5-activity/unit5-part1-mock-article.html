Early Amplification Versus Waitlist for Adults with Mild-to-Moderate Age-Related Hearing Loss: A Randomized Controlled Trial
Results (excerpt)
A total of 200 adults (ages 55–75) with bilateral mild-to-moderate sensorineural hearing loss were randomized to either immediate hearing aid fitting (n = 100) or a 6-month waitlist control (n = 100). The primary outcome was self-reported communication improvement on the Hearing Handicap Inventory for the Elderly (HHIE), defined as a reduction of ≥10 points at 6 months.
At 6 months, 55 of 100 patients (55%) in the early amplification group achieved meaningful communication improvement, compared with 25 of 100 patients (25%) in the waitlist group. The early amplification group was 2.20 times as likely to achieve meaningful improvement compared to the waitlist group (RR = 2.20, 95% CI: 1.52 to 3.19). The odds of improvement were 3.67 times higher in the amplification group (OR = 3.67, 95% CI: 2.01 to 6.68). The absolute risk reduction was 30.0 percentage points (ARR = 30.0%, 95% CI: 18.1% to 41.9%), yielding a number needed to treat of 4 (NNT = 4, 95% CI: 3 to 6).
