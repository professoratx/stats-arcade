In this tool, you'll explore whether repetitive hand use at work increases the odds of developing carpal tunnel syndrome (CTS). The Odds Ratio (OR) appears frequently in journal articles, especially in case-control studies and logistic regression. It measures the same basic idea as RR — is there an association? — but uses a different formula. Recall from the mock article: the RR was 2.20 but the OR was 3.67 from the same data. This tool shows you when and why they diverge.
1
Start in the “Risk Factor” context with the default scenario.
2
Move the sliders to change outcome rates and watch how OR and RR compare side by side.
3
Pay attention to the divergence indicator — it tells you how much the OR exaggerates compared to the RR.
