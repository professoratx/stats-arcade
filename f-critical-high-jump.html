How do we determine if an F-statistic is significant?

The same high jump concept we used for the t-test but applied to the F-test (ANOVA/RMANOVA). The purple jumper is your F-statistic, and the bar is the F-critical value. Adjust the number of groups and total participants and see how things change.

Interpretation: If the jumper clears the bar, the F-test result is statistically significant — at least one group is different from the others. If it does not clear the bar, there is no evidence of a difference among the groups.
