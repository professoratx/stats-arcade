What is an odds ratio and when will you see one?

The odds ratio is one of the most common statistics in clinical research. It looks like a relative risk — but it isn't. Drag the sliders and watch what happens when you compare them side by side.

How to use this tool: Start with the headline below. Then choose a clinical scenario, adjust the numbers, and watch the Odds Ratio and Relative Risk update side by side. Pay attention to when they agree — and when they don't.
÷Odds Ratio (OR)▼
The odds ratio compares the odds of an outcome in one group to the odds in another. Think of it as: how much more likely is this outcome in one group vs. the other, measured in odds?
How to read it:OR = 1 — No difference between groupsOR > 1 — Higher odds in Group 1OR < 1 — Lower odds in Group 1
↔When will I see OR vs. RR?▼
OR appears in case-control studies and logistic regression models.
RR appears in RCTs, cohort studies, and prevention research.
Key rule:When the outcome is rare (under 10%), OR and RR are very similar.When the outcome is common, OR exaggerates the effect.
