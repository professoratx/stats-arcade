What is a confidence interval?

A 95% confidence interval is like casting a net to catch the true population mean. Adjust the sample size and see how the width of your net changes.

Interpretation: A larger sample gives you a narrower net (a more precise estimate of where the population mean is), while a smaller sample gives you a wider net (a less precise estimate of where the population mean is). A wide CI means less certainty about where the population mean (i.e., the golden egg) falls. Try different sample sizes and see if your net catches the golden egg (the population mean).
